✈️ A trip to Manali: My Experience in the Himalayas

Last weekend, I finally ticked Manali off my travel bucket list—and it was worth every second! Nestled in the
Himalayas, Manali is a mix of breathtaking landscapes, cozy cafes, and adventurous vibes.

🌄 Day 1: Arrival & First Impressions

The winding roads were tiring, but as soon as I saw the snow-capped peaks, I forgot everything. The crisp
mountain air instantly refreshed me. I checked into a small homestay overlooking apple orchards—it felt like
something out of a postcard.

🚵 Day 2: Rohtang Pass Road Trip

I took a road trip to Rohtang Pass, and the journey itself was breathtaking. The snowy landscapes, waterfalls
along the way, and winding mountain roads made it unforgettable. Playing in the snow felt like being a kid
again!

🛍️ Day 3: Shopping in Mall Road

Manali’s Mall Road is buzzing with life. I bought some warm woollen scarves, handmade souvenirs, and local
jams. The street food—especially hot corn and Maggi noodles—was a treat in the chilly weather.

🕉️ Day 4: Hidimba Devi Temple Visit

Surrounded by tall cedar trees, the Hidimba Devi Temple was peaceful and spiritual. The wooden architecture
stood out beautifully against the green forest backdrop. It was a quiet place to reflect.

🌲 Day 5: Trek to Jogini Waterfalls

On my last day, I hiked to Jogini Waterfalls near Vashisht Village. The trail was scenic, with apple orchards
and small streams along the way. Sitting near the waterfall and feeling the cool mist was the perfect
goodbye to Manali.

🍜 Foodie Heaven

Manali is a paradise for food lovers. From steaming momos to hot chocolate in cute cafés, everything felt
extra delicious in the cold weather. My personal favorite was the riverside café near Old Manali—it had the
most calming view.

🏔️ Adventure Time

On Day 2, I tried paragliding in Solang Valley. At first, I was nervous, but once I was in the air, the view of the
valley below was surreal. Truly a memory for life!

🌌 Night Under the Stars

Before leaving, I sat by the Beas River at night. The sound of flowing water mixed with a sky full of stars—it
was the perfect ending to my trip.
